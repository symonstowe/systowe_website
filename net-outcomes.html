Net Outcomes

An NHL analytics project

Net Outcomes is an NHL analytics project focused on player, goalie, and team evaluation using public NHL API data. The site combines on-ice and personal statistics into interpretable rankings, scorecards, and team-level views designed to be updated throughout the season.

Metrics are weighted heavily toward the current season, while the previous three seasons are used to assign certainty. All analysis excludes empty-net goals unless otherwise specified, and rankings are scaled based on certainty and stability.

Why I started this project

When looking at expected goals (xG) metrics in hockey I felt that there were often some gaps between what the data was showing and what the game outcomes were. A lot of the discrepancy between xG and what I was seeing could be corrected for by removing effects of empty net goals, but more interesting stories started to appear when I started fitting corrections based on situation importance and shot outcomes. I am felt that there were interesting stories in some of the statistics. For example: a certain player might have a great xG differential but every time they step on the ice their goalie performs worse. They may generate a lot of chances - but what are the net outcomes of their time on the ice?

Some of the questions I was curious to answer with this analysis:

  • Can we design lines around specific player strengths and weaknesses to maximize a team’s chance of success?
  • Can we pick an optimal setup for a national team based on player metrics?
  • How do our estimates of what the lines “should” be correspond with actual management and coaching decisions?
  • When a player we feel is underrated and they do get more ice time, does our prediction hold up?
